Key Findings

Our comprehensive analysis revealed that while traditional CNNs still maintain their position as the top performer for image classification tasks, the Convolution Tables approach shows remarkable promise with only a 2% accuracy gap despite its architectural simplicity. FCNNs, while less accurate overall, demonstrated competitive performance on certain classes that have distinctive global features.

Model Analysis

The confusion matrices indicate that CNNs excel at capturing local features that distinguish similar objects, while CTs offer a good balance between global and local feature extraction. FCNNs struggled with classes that rely heavily on spatial relationships but performed well when color distribution and overall shape were sufficient discriminators.

Future Directions

The promising performance of Convolution Tables suggests several interesting avenues for future research. Hybrid architectures that combine the strengths of CNNs and CTs could potentially achieve better performance with lower computational costs. Additionally, exploring the application of these models to other computer vision tasks beyond classification would help establish their broader utility in the deep learning ecosystem.
