Model Implementation

We implemented three distinct neural network architectures to evaluate their performance on the CIFAR datasets. Each model was designed to represent its category's typical structure and optimization approaches.

Model Architectures

Each network was carefully designed to ensure fair comparison while maintaining the essential characteristics of their respective architectural approaches.

Training Protocol

All models were trained and validated on the CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-100 datasets using identical preprocessing steps and optimization configurations. Training was conducted for 100 epochs using stochastic gradient descent with a momentum of 0.9, weight decay of 5e-4, and learning rate of 0.01 with a step decay schedule. Data augmentation techniques included random cropping and horizontal flipping.

For fair comparison, we ensured that each model had comparable parameter counts and utilized the same hardware setup for training and inference tests. All experiments were repeated three times with different random seeds to ensure statistical significance of the results.
